# CallPilot — Project Summary

*The Negotiator · ElevenLabs × Hack-Nation. ~200 words for the jury.*

---

Every phone-priced market runs on the same unfairness: the same 45-mile move gets quoted anywhere from **$1,158 to $6,506** — a **5.6× spread** for identical work — and the only defense is calling 5–8 companies, describing your job identically each time, and haggling. Almost nobody has the hours. Sight-unseen phone quotes end 40% higher than promised (FMCSA).

**CallPilot is a voice agent that does the calling for you.** It runs the same three beats every time:

- **Estimate** — a voice interview (ElevenLabs Agents) *and* document/photo upload (OpenAI vision) build one structured job spec you confirm; it's then read verbatim on every call.
- **Call** — the agent negotiates real, distinct counterparties (a tough negotiator, a stonewaller, a lowballer), extracting itemized, comparable quotes through a schema'd tool. The negotiations are genuine and unscripted — the price moves on the merits, not a script.
- **Close** — it calls the winners back citing the *actual* cheapest quote as leverage (never a fabricated number), applies red-flag rules (30%-below-market, hidden deposits), and returns a ranked report with transcript evidence and a plain-language recommendation.

It discloses it's an AI, never invents inventory or bids, and ends every call in a structured outcome. Retargeting from movers to auto-repair or medical bills is **one YAML file** — the whole system is config-driven.
